Two-Day Course

10 lecture-modules over two days · up to 6 pods of 3–5 · on paper & whiteboard (no sandbox required)

unlisted · share by link only
The frame. The whole course runs inside one fictional bank, Meridian National , standing up its GenAI portfolio. Each pod owns one live use case for both days and defends it at a mock AI Risk Committee in the capstone. Day 1 is build your case ; Day 2 is defend it under fire as curveballs land.

The spine

Eight modules are short lectures feeding a team exercise. Two — The Eval Gap and Adversarial Testing — run as studios , taught entirely through the exercise (the base-rate computation and the red-team relay), because for this audience doing beats hearing. Modules 7+8 and 9+10 are paired into single slots to fit two days.

Exercise playbook

Cold-open provocation Day 1 · 9:00 · 15 min

Open with a single contrarian claim; pods vote agree/disagree before any lecture. Reveal the tension the two days will test. Reuse as a 60-second warm-up before each module.

Output: a visible split in the room you can call back to later.
Exercise A · Scope triage Day 1 · M1 · 25 min

Hand each pod five short use-case descriptions. They sort each in / out of SR 26-2 scope and write the one sentence that decides it. Surface the GenAI exclusion as the trap.

Output: a sorted list + the deciding sentence per case.
Exercise B · Harness teardown Day 1 · M2 · 25 min

Give every pod the same one-page flawed harness sketch (e.g. no termination contract, unbounded tool access). Pods mark the failure mode and the smallest fix.

Output: one circled failure + one-line fix.
Exercise C · A2A threat model Day 1 · M3 · 25 min

Pods draw their own case as agents + privileges on the whiteboard, then mark the trust boundary and the highest-privilege action.

Output: a boundary line and the one action that needs the most scrutiny.
Studio · The base-rate trap Day 2 · M5 · 50 min (taught as the lecture)

Give the prevalence and the judge's TPR/FPR. Pods compute PPV by hand, then the effective sensitivity (judge × reviewer). The math is the lecture. ClearList's numbers are on its brief.

Output: a PPV figure and "zero flags ≠ zero failures" in their own words.
Studio · Red-team relay Day 2 · M6 · 60 min (taught as the lecture)

Round 1: each pod writes 3 attacks against its own case. Round 2: pass clockwise; the receiving pod classifies (OWASP/ASI) and designs a control. Round 3: brief back.

Output: 3 attacks + 3 controls per case, classified.
Exercise · Knight Capital / override signal Day 2 · M7+M8 · 25 min

Reenact the 97-emails failure briefly, then each pod designs one runtime oversight signal for its case: override rate, drift cadence, or a deskilling proxy.

Output: one signal, its threshold, and who watches it.
Steelman-swap Recurring · ~10 min

A pod must argue the position opposite to its instinct (e.g. "runtime monitoring is enough"). Forces honest engagement with the counterargument the course keeps raising.

Output: the strongest version of the view they disagree with.
Module quiz competition Day 1 close · 30 min

Run the blog's existing module tests as a team game — read questions aloud or print them. Leaderboard on the whiteboard. No devices required.

Output: a leaderboard going into Day 2.
Capstone · Gallery walk Day 2 · 15:40 · 50 min

Each pod posts its case, its build, the curveball it absorbed, and its go/no-go recommendation. The room rotates as a mock AI Risk Committee and challenges each. This is effective challenge, performed.

Output: a committee verdict per case + the strongest challenge each pod received.
